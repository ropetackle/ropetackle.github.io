1 Berringtons Lane, Rainford, St Helens, (Grid Reference SJ 489989) is the isolated cottage where Grandma and Grandpa Davies lived. Grandpa Davies was living here when he died in 1956.

Alan, Brian and myself spent many a happy time here. We used to go and visit them almost every Saturday. In the garden there was an old pig-sty with a loft above. This used to make a marvelous den. We used to play for hours in the garden and in the fields around the house.

Grandpa grew lots of vegetables, raspberries and other soft fruit and we always used to stuff ourselves on them when they were in season. If you have never eaten a miniature soft brown bread roll with the centre removed and filled instead with raspberries, you don't know what good living is!

It was fun when you wanted to go to the lavatory. It was an earth closet in an outhouse attached to the house. To get to it you had to go into the yard. There was no flushing water and for toilet paper there were cut up pages of the "News Chronicle" newspaper! I bet it isn't like that now.

I can still remember our winter evenings there. At 5 o'clock we would be sitting by the fire eating the waffles that Grandma used to make on the top of the kitchen range using an old fashioned waffling iron, and watching Grandpa check his football coupons as the results were read out over the radio. I don't think he ever won anything, but he always lived in hope. (Robin Marshall)
