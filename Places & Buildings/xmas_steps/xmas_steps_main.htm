Christmas Steps, Bristol is the area of Bristol in which Alice Williams, and possibly her mother Louisa Williams, are believed to have originated.
 
Christmas Steps is the name of a narrow pedestrian street in the centre of Bristol running between Lower Park Row, Lewins Mead and Colston Street. It used to lead to Christmas Street.
 
One theory is that it got its name from Knifesmith Street where the knifesmiths worked.
 
In the C17th the steps were used as a short cut into the centre of the city from the north bank of the River Frome and in 1669 they were re-stepped by Jonathan Blackwell, a wealthy wine merchant, because they had become hazardous.
 
At one time they were known as Lunsfords Stairs, after a Royalist Officer of that name who was killed there during the siege of Bristol in the Civil War.
 
The Steps still exist and its cobbled street is now lined with craft and book shops. It is part of the Bristol tourist trail. I am sure it looks a lot different from the days when Alice and Louisa lived there.
(Robin Marshall)
