Born: About 1879 or 1880 in Liverpool
 
 Parents: Edward Davies and Margaret Pennill
 
Brothers and Sisters: William,  Caroline,  Marion,  Thomas,  Georgina,  Winifred

Edward Davies, known as "Chip", was the fifth child of Edward Davies and his wife, Margaret Pennill

He was born in Liverpool in about 1889 or 1890.

He had six brothers and sisters. These were:-

William, Caroline,  Marion,  Thomas,  Georgina,  and Winifred

The earliest reference to him is in the 1891 Census. He was 1 year old and living at the house of his  maternal Grandmother, Marion Pennill, at 9 Elm Grove, West Derby, Liverpool with his parents, brothers William and Thomas, sisters Caroline and Marion, and his Aunt Georgina and her husband.

Edward is next mentioned in the 1901 Census. He was now 11, still at school and still living with his parents at 9 Elm Grove, West Derby, Liverpool.

He is thought to have died in about 1927 or 1928. When his sister Marion died during War War II, Edward's widow and her children moved into the Davies family house at 137, Alderson Road, Liverpool.
