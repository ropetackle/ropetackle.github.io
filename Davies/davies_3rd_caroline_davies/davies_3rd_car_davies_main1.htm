Born: Between 1879 and 1881 in Liverpool
 
 Parents: Edward Davies and Margaret Pennill
 
Brothers and Sisters: William,  Marion,  Thomas,  Edward,  Georgina,  Winifred

Caroline Davies was the second child of Edward Davies, a Bricklayer, and his wife, Margaret Pennill

She was born in Liverpool in between 1879 and 1881.

She had three brothers and three sisters. These were:-

William, Marion,  Thomas,  Edward,  Georgina,  and Winifred

The earliest reference to her is in the 1881 Census. She was 1 year old and living with her parents and brother William at the house of her maternal Grandparents, William and Marion Pennill, at 9 Elm Grove, West Derby Liverpool.
Also living there were her Uncle, Alfred Pennill and her Aunt, Georgina Pennill as well as a Lodger.

She and her parents were still living with her Grandmother, Marion Pennill, at 9 Elm Grove when the 1891 Census was taken. She was now 10 and had a sister, Marion, and two more brothers, Thomas and Edward. Also living with them were her Aunt Georgina and her husband

When the 1901 Census was taken, Caroline was still living at 9 Elm Grove, West Derby, Liverpool with her parents and and her three brothers and three sisters. She was now 21, working as a Bread Shop Assistant.

Her niece, Peggy Davies, says that Caroline married a Roman Catholic and was treated as an "outcast" from the family by her sister Marion because of this. Following Caroline's marriage, Marion never spoke to her sister again nor would she talk about her!

One of Caroline's children, Bill, joined the RAF during World War II as a pilot. After a tour of duty in England and another training pilots in Canada he was killed over Holland while flying as a night-fighter pilot.
