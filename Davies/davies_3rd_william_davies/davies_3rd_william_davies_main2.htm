After their marriage in 1909 William Davies and his wife Ethelina first lived near the Hospital in a terrace house in Edge Street, Nut Grove, St Helens and it was here that their first child, William Alfred, (known as Bill) was born in 1910.

However when they married the Hospital was building some new houses on the edge of the Hospital grounds on the West side of Rainhill Road, Nut Grove. When these were completed, they moved into one of them.

This house, at Upper Palatine Villas, Rainhill Road, was one of a row of substantial semi-detached houses. They were apparently among the first houses in the area to have electricity. The houses no longer exist, newer houses having been built on the site although, in November 1999, the original gate-posts of the front paths were still there.

It was at Palatine Villas that their second child, Margaret Ethel, (known as Peggy) was born on 19th January 1913.

By 1937, when Peggy Davies married, William had changed his job, although he still worked at the Hospital. He was now the Chief Officer of the Hospital's Fire Brigade and they lived in a cottage in the Hospital grounds on the East side of Rainhill Road. This was known as Fireman’s Cottage. There is now no trace of the Cottage.

He continued to work as the Chief Officer of the Hospital's fire brigade. until he retired.

When he retired he and Ethelina went to live at 158 Speakman Road, St Helens.

After living there a number of years, and latterly sharing the house with his son Bill and Bill's family, he and Ethelina moved to an isolated Cottage, 1 Berringtons Lane, near Rainford where he lived here until his death.

He died from Cerebral Thrombosis in the Providence Hospital, St Helens on 20th April 1956, aged 77.
