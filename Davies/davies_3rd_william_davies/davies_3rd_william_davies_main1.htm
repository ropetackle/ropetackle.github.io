William Davies In 1937

Relationship: William Davies is a Great-Great-Grandfather of Chloe and Daniel Marshall.
 
Born: Between 1877 and 1879 in Liverpool
 
Parents: Edward Davies and Margaret Pennill
 
Brothers and Sisters: Caroline,  Marion,  Thomas,  Edward,  Georgina,  Winifred
 
Wife: Ethelina O'Connor
 
Children: William Alfred (Bill) and Margaret Ethel (Peggy)
 
Died: 20th April 1956

William Davies was the eldest child of Edward Davies, a Bricklayer, and his wife Margaret Pennill

He was born in Liverpool between 1877 and 1879.

He had six brothers and sisters. These were:-

Caroline, Marion, Thomas, Edward, Georgina, and Winifred

The earliest reference to William is in the 1881 Census. He was 2 year old and living with his parents and sister Caroline at 9 Elm Grove, West Derby Liverpool, the house of his maternal Grandparents, William and Marion Pennill.
Also living with them were his Uncle, Alfred Pennill, his Aunt Georgina Pennill and a lodger, Margaret Stephenson, a 57 year old Tailoress.

William and his parents were still living with his Grandmother, Marion Pennill, at 9 Elm Grove when the 1891 Census was taken, his Grandfather, William Pennill, having died. He was now 12 years old and had another sister, Marion, and two brothers, Thomas and Edward.
Also living there were his Aunt Georgina and her husband, William Stothart.

William was apparently quite clever and when at school won a scholarship. Unfortunately he had to leave school to start earning a living and couldn't take advantage of it. When he left school he trained as a Plumber and Gas Fitter. 

When the 1901 Census was taken he was still living with his parents at  9 Elm Grove, West Derby, Liverpool He was 22 years old and working as a Gas Fitter. He now had two more sisters, Georgina and Winifred.

While he was working as a Gas Fitter he was involved in a gas explosion and suffered serious burns to his face and as a result spent a long time in hospital. It was because of these burns that he grew his beard.

He later became a Male Nurse at Rainhill Mental Hospital - why he did this isn't known but it must have been safer than being a Gas Fitter!

It was while he was working as a Male Nurse at Rainhill Mental Hospital that he met Ethelina O'Connor who was also working there as a Nurse. They became engaged and on the 28th April 1909 they were married at Christ Church, the Parish Church of Eccleston.
