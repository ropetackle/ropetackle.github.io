Relationship: Edward Davies is a Great-Great-Great-Grandfather of Chloe and Daniel Marshall
 
Born: 1850 at Amlwch, Anglesey, North Wales
 
Parents: Thomas Davies and Winifred Parry
 
Brothers and Sisters: John,  Margaret,  Robert,  Thomas,  Winifred,  and William
 
Wife: Margaret Ann Pennill
 
Children: William,  Caroline,  Marion,  Thomas,  Edward,  Georgina,  Winifred

Edward Davies was born at New Street,  Amlwch, Anglesey on 2nd July 1850. He was the youngest son of Thomas Davies and Winifred Parry. His father was a Bricklayer and both Edward's father and mother came from Newmarket, (now known as Trelawnyd), Fintshire. It is thought that they moved to Amlwch from Newmarket some time between 1847 and 1850 as all his brothers and sisters were born in Newmarket.

He had at least four brothers and two sisters. These were:-

John, Margaret, Robert, Thomas, Winifred and William.

He married Margaret Ann Pennill and they had 7 children. These were:-

William,  Caroline,  Marion,  Thomas,  Edward,  Georgina,  and Winifred

The earliest reference to Edward is his Birth Certificate. When he was born his parents were living in New Street, Amlwch and his father worked as a Bricklayer.
His father does not seem to have been able to write as in the Birth Register he "made his mark" with an X instead of signing the Register.

Edward is next mentioned in the 1851 Census. He was now 8 months old and the family were still in Amlwch and his father was still a Bricklayer.

The next mentioned of Edward is in the 1881 Census. He was now 30 years old and working as a Bricklayer in Liverpool. He was married and living with his wife Margaret and their two eldest children, William and Caroline, with his father and mother-in-law, William and Marion Pennill, at 9 Elm Grove, Edge Hill, Liverpool. Also living in the house were his brother-in-law Alfred and sister-in-law Georgina as well as a lodger, Margaret Stephenson, a 57 year old Tailoress.

He was still living at 9 Elm Grove when the 1891 Census was taken. He was now 40 and his occupation is given as that of a Bricksetter. His family had grown and he and Margaret had three more children, Marion, Thomas and Edward. The house belonged to his mother-in-law Marion Pennill and his sister-in-law Georgina and her husband were also lived there.

9 Elm Grove was still his home when the 1901 Census was taken. He was now 50 years old and still employed as a Bricklayer. His family had again grown and there were two more children, Georgina and Winifred. His mother-in-law, Marion, seems to have died as Edward was now the Head of the Household.

When his son William married in 1909 the Marriage Certificate described Edward's occupation as being that of a Mason.
