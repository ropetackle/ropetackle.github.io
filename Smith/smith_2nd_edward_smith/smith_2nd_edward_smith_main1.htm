Relationship: Edward Thomas Smith is a Great-Great-Great-Grandfather of Faye, Jenny and Kate Lloyd and of Chloe and Daniel Marshall
 
Born: 1838 in Helston, Cornwall
 
Parents: John Smith and Elizabeth Pike
 
Brothers and Sisters: Harriet,  John
 
Wife: Elizabeth Smith
 
Children: Elizabeth Ann,  Emma Jane,  Edward John Thomas, Richard,  Harriet Katey
 
Died: 1899

Edward Thomas Smith was born in Helston, Cornwall on 11th March 1838. He was the son of John Smith, a Tailor, and Elizabeth Pike.

Edward had at least one sister and one brother. These were:

Harriet and John

Edward married Elizabeth Smith and they had 5 children. These were:-

Elizabeth Ann, Emma Jane, Edward John Thomas, Richard and Harriet Katey

The earliest reference to Edward is his Birth Certificate. He was born at Wendron Street, Helston on 11th March 1838. When he was born his Father was employed as a Tailor.

Edward is next mentioned in the 1841 Census. He was 3 years old and living with his parents and older sister in Wendron Street.

When the 1881 Census was taken Edward was living at Vernon Place, Falmouth. He was 43 years old, married and, like his father worked as a Tailor. By now he and his wife Elizabeth had 5 children.

By the time the 1891 Census was taken, Edward and his family had moved to live in Meneage Street, Helston. He is thought to have been the "Headman" at Basil Thomas's, a Gentlemen's Outfitters in Coinage Hall Street, Helston

Edward died at Meneage Street on 17th October 1899 from Peritonitis. He was 61 years old although the Death Certificate gives his age as 64. He is buried in Helston Cemetery.

Some information about Edward is contained in a letter sent by one of his Grandaughters, Olive Treloar, to her cousin Edward John Kingdon in 1977. The letter also contains some amusing and interesting information on the Smith family and life in Falmouth towards the end of the C19th.
