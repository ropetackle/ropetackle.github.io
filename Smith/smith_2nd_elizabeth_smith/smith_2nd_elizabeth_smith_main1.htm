Relationship: Elizabeth Smith is a Great-Great-Great-Grandmother of Faye, Jenny and Kate Lloyd and of Chloe and Daniel Marshall.
 
Born: 1840 in Cadgwith, Cornwall
 
Parents: James Smith and Matilda Smith (maiden name unknown)
 
Sister: Jane
 
Husband: Edward Thomas Smith
 
Children: Elizabeth,  Emma,  Edward,  Richard and Katey
 
Died: 1916

Elizabeth Smith was born in Cadgwith, in the Parish of Ruan Minor, Cornwall in 1840. She was the daughter of James Smith, a Fisherman, and his wife Matilda.

Elizabeth had at least one older sister, Jane.

Elizabeth married Edward Thomas Smith, a Master Tailor and they had 5 children. These were:-

Elizabeth Ann, Emma Jane, Edward John Thomas, Richard, and Harriet Katey

It is thought that she met Edward in Falmouth, as she appears to have been trained in the Tailoring Industry.

The earliest reference to Elizabeth is in the 1841 Census. She was 10 months old and living with her parents and older sister Jane in Cadgwith. also living with them was a 75 year old lodger, Henry Trewise.

Elizabeth is next mentioned in 1877 when her daughter Harriet Katey was born. She was living at Waterloo Road, Falmouth.

When the 1881 Census was taken Elizabeth was still living in Falmouth in Vernon Place. She was 41 years old and worked as a Seam Machinist.

By the time the 1891 Census was taken Elizabeth was 52 years old and she and her family had moved from Falmouth to her husband's home town of Helston. They were living in Meneage Street.

In 1899 Elizabeth's husband died. This meant that the family were no longer as prosperous and Elizabeth had to take in a lodger to help financially. This was why James Kingdon, an Engine Cleaner employed by the Great Western Railway, who had been moved from Exeter to Helston took up lodgings with her. It is also likely that it was because of her financial position that she and her family moved from Meneage Street to 38 St Johns Road Helston.

When the 1901 Census was taken Elizabeth was 63 years old and living at 38 St Johns Road. Living there with her were her two daughters, Elizabeth Ann and Harriet Katey. Also in the house was her lodger, James Kingdon.

Elizabeth died at Sea View Terrace, Church Street, Helston on 22nd May 1916 aged 75. The cause of death was given as Hemiplegia. She is buried in Helston Cemetery.

According to her grandson Jack Kingdon she was tall and angular with a strong personality.

An account of Jack Kingdon’s embarrassing experiences at her funeral is given in his diary entry for the 4th April 1980.
A second account of the funeral is given in a letter sent by Olive Treloar to her cousin Edward John Kingdon in 1977. This letter also contains some amusing and interesting information on the Smith family and life in Falmouth towards the end of the C19th.
