Relationship: Elizabeth Pike is a Great-Great-Great-Great-Grandmother of Faye, Jenny and Kate Lloyd and of Chloe and Daniel Marshall.
 
Born: Penryn, Cornwall between 1799 and 1801.
 
Parents: Thomas Pike and Elizabeth (maiden name unknown)
 
Sisters and Brother: Ann,  Diana,  Thomas
 
Husband: John Smith
 
Children: Harriett,  Edward,  John

Elizabeth Pike was born in Penryn, Cornwall between 1799 and 1801. She is thought to have been a daughter of Thomas Pike, a Tallow Chandler, and his wife Elizabeth, a Straw Bonnet Maker.

Elizabeth is thought to have had at least two sisters and a brother. These were:

Ann, Diana and Thomas

Elizabeth married John Smith, a tailor and they had at least three children. These were:-

Harriett, Edward Thomas and John.

The reasons for thinking that Elizabeth was the daughter of Thomas and Elizabeth Pike are:

(1) Both she and Thomas Pike's wife were called Elizabeth
(2) Thomas Pike's family were the only Pikes in Penryn in 1841
(3) Elizabeth was 40 when the 1841 Census was taken and this would tie in with the ages of  Thomas and his wife who were both 65 years old at that time, and the ages of their children - Ann 30, Diana 25 and Thomas 20.
(3) Elizabeth's husband, John Smith, was a Tailor as was the son of Thomas and Elizabeth. It is possible that John and Thomas either worked together or Thomas had been John's Apprentice and this was how she met John.

The first reference to Elizabeth is in the Birth Certificate of her son Edward Thomas who was born on 11th March 1838. She was then living in Wendron Street, Helston, Cornwall.

 When the 1841 Census was taken Elizabeth was still living in Wendron Street with her husband and two children, Harriett and Edward. She was 40 years old.

Elizabeth was still at Wendron Street when the 1861 Census was taken. She was now 61 years old and a Widow. Living with her was her 15 year old son, John.
