Richard H Smith was born in Falmouth in either 1874 or 1875 and was the youngest son of Edward Thomas Smith, a Master Tailor, and his wife Elizabeth.

He had three sisters and one brother. These were:-

  • Elizabeth Ann
  • Emma Jane
  • Edward John Thomas
  • Harriet Katey

He died young and is buried in Helston Cemetery.

According to his niece Olive Treloar, the Smith family lived in Falmouth "on the Terrace above Jacob’s Ladder". His parents later moved from Falmouth to Helston where his father was the "Headman" at Basil Thomas's, a Gentlemen's Outfitters in Coinage Hall Street.

DETAILS FROM 1881 CENSUS

The first reference to Richard is in the 1891 Census. He was 16 years old, working as a Blacksmith's Apprentice and living with his parents and his two sisters Emma Jane and Harriet Katey in Meneage Street, Helston.

He subsequently worked at a Foundry in Falmouth.

Most of the above information about him is taken from a letter sent by Olive Treloar to her cousin Edward John Kingdon in 1977. The letter also contains some amusing and interesting information on the Smith family and life in Falmouth towards the end of the C19th.
