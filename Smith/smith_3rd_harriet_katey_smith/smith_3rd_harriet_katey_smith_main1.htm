Harriet Katey Smith was born in Falmouth between 1875 and 1879. She was a daughter of Edward Thomas Smith and his wife Elizabeth.

She had two sisters and two brothers. These were:-

  • Elizabeth Ann
  • Richard
  • Emma Jane
  • Edward John Thomas

She had one daughter, Violet.

According to Katey's niece Olive, the Smith family lived in Falmouth "on the Terrace above Jacob’s Ladder". Her parents later moved from Falmouth to Helston where her father was the "Headman" at Basil Thomas's, a Gentlemen's Outfitters in Coinage Hall Street.

DETAILS FROM 1881 CENSUS

The earliest reference to Katey is the 1891 Census. She was 15 years old, still at school and living with her parents in Meneage Street, Helston.

Katey is next mentioned in the 1901 Census. She was living with her parents at 38 St John's Road, Helston. Her age is given in the Census Return as 22, not 25 as would be expected if her age given in the 1891 Census was correct.
The Census Return says that she was a Tailoress but according to her niece Olive Treloar it would be more accurate to describe her as a Trouser Hand.

Most of the above information about her is taken from a letter sent by her niece Olive Treloar to her cousin Edward John Kingdon in 1977. The letter also contains some amusing and interesting information on the Smith family and life in Falmouth towards the end of the C19th.
