Olive Treloar was born in Helston in 1906 and was the daughter of Emma Jane Smith and Alfred Treloar.

Olive had one brother, Norman.

In 1905 Olive married Gordon Williams, a farmer from Bodilly near Coverack Bridges which is about 2 miles north-east of Helston on the B3297.

Olive and Gordon had a daughter, Pauline.

Olive's father came from an old Cornish family and was a miner who spent at least 20 years in the gold fields of South Africa, Australia and America.

Olive and Gordon farmed at Bodilly for 15 years. They then moved to Little Lidgey, Penryn where they farmed for a further 16 years. In 1957 they gave up farming and moved to 5 Lady Street, Helston.

Olive's daughter Pauline married a Hawkins and in 1975 was living in Falmouth.

In 1977 Olive sent to her cousin Edward John Kingdon a wonderful letter, "written" with a "Cornish accent".
This letter contains an amusing account of her reaction to the funeral of Elizabeth Smith, her maternal grandmother as well as other interesting information on her maternal grandparents and their family and on events in Falmouth at the end of the C19th.
