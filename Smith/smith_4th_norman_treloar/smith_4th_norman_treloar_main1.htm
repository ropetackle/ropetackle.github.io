Norman Treloar was born in Helston and was the son of Emma Jane Smith and Alfred Treloar.

He had one sister, Olive.

His wife was called Annie

His father came from an old Cornish family and was a miner who spent at least 20 years in the gold fields of South Africa, Australia and America.

Norman was an Engine Driver with the Great Western Railway, and drove the trains on the branch line from Gwinear Road, which was a junction on the main line to Penzance, to Helston. Because the branch line was so rural he used to allow his cousin, Edward John Kingdon, to travel on the foot plate of the engine even though it was strictly forbidden and the penalty was instant dismissal.
