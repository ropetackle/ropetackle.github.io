Relationship: Louisa Williams is a Great-Great-Great-Great-Grandmother of Chloe and Daniel Marshall.
 
Born: Bristol in about 1832 or 1833
 
Husband: Richard Williams
 
Children: Mary,  Harold  and Alice Louisa

Nothing is known about Louisa's parents. Neither is her maiden name known.

Louisa was born in Bristol, possibly in the area known as Christmas Steps.

Louisa married Richard Williams, a Shopkeeper and they had at least 3 children. These were:-

Mary,  Harold  and Alice Louisa

The earliest reference to her is in the 1881 Census. She was 48, working as a Dressmaker and living at 18 Alfred Cross Street, Leeds. Although the Census Return describes her as being married and not as a widow there is no mention of any husband and she is given as the Head of the Household.
Living with her at Alfred Cross Street were her daughter Alice, Alice's husband James O'Connor and their two children, Ethelina and James.

When the 1891 Census was taken she had moved and was living on her own at 14 Bywater Sreet, Leeds. She was now 57 years old and is described as being a widow. Although her profession is given as being that of Dressmaker, her employment status is shown as being "neither employed or unemployed". This would seem to support the belief that she had her own dressmaking business.

Louisa is next mentioned in the 1901 Census. She was now 68, no longer working and living at 15 Scarsdale Street, Leeds. Also living with her was her grandaughter, Ethelina O'Connor and her youngest grandson, Joseph. The reason for this was that her daughter Alice had died in November 1900. Alice's only daughter, Ethelina, and her 1year old son had gone to live with their grandmother while Alice's husband lived nearby with the rest of the children.
Also living in Louisa's house was a lodger  James McGowan, a 44 year old Farrier from Scotland.

Some time after the 1901 Census, Louisa and her granddaughter Ethelina moved to live with Louisa's daughter Mary somewhere in the centre of Liverpool, in the area between Mount Pleasant and Lime Street.

According to her great-grandaughter Margaret Ethel Davies, Louisa had her own dressmaking business and employed several girls.
