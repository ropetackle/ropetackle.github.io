Relationship: James Daniel O'Connor is a Great-Great-Great-Grandfather of Chloe and Daniel Marshall.
 
Born: Between 1853 and 1858
 
Parents: Daniel O'Connor (Mother unknown)
 
Wife: Alice Louisa Williams
 
Children: Ethelina,   James,   Edward,   Robert,   Alfred Hartley,   Claude, and Joseph Somerville

James Daniel O'Connor was the son of Daniel O'Connor, a Mechanic, and his wife.
There seems to be some confusion about where and when he was born. According to the 1881 and 1891 Census he was born in Leeds in either 1857 or 1858. However, according to the 1901 Census he was born in Ireland in about 1853 or 1854.

James married Alice Louisa Williams and they had 6 or 7 children. These were:-

Ethelina,   James,   Edward,   Robert,   Alfred Hartley,   Claude, and Joseph Somerville

The doubt about the number of children is because the eldest, Ethelina, may not have been his child. When James married Alice, Ethelina was already 2 years old.

The earliest reference to James Daniel is when he married Alice in Leeds on 10th April 1880. Both he and Alice were living with her mother, Louisa Williams, at 18 Alfred Cross Street, Leeds. At the time he was employed as a Forgeman.
It is possible that he met Alice because he was lodging with her mother.

When the the 1881 Census was taken James was 23 years old, working as a Smith Forge Man and still living at 18 Alfred Cross Road, Leeds with his mother-in-law Louisa Williams, wife Alice Louisa and her two children, Ethelina and James.

When his son, Alfred Hartley, was born in January 1888 he, Alice and their family had moved to 16 Bywater Street, Leeds and he was working as a Mill Labourer.

James was still living in Bywater Street, although at No.4 not 16, when the 1891 Census was taken. He was now 33 years old and he was employed as a Mechanical Engineer. By now he and Alice had 6 children.

In November 1900 his life underwent a dramatic change. Alice died, aged 40, from Breast Cancer leaving him with their youngest son Joseph, who was only a year old. When she died his occupation was given on her Death Certificate as that of a Wood Moulder.

When the 1901 Census was taken, four months after Alice's death, the problem had been sorted. James continued to live at 4 Bywater Street with 3 of his sons, Robert, Alfred and Claude while Ethelina and his son Joseph went to live with his mother-in-law, Louisa Williams, who lived nearby in Scarsdale Street.
The 1901 Census return gives James age as 47 and his birthplace as Ireland. He was working as a Joiner.

The last mention of James is when Ethelina married on the 28th April 1909. Her Marriage Certificate gives him as her father and his occupation as that of Engineer.
