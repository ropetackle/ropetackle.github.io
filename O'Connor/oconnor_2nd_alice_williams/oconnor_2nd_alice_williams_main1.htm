Relationship: Alice Louisa Williams is a Great-Great-Great-Grandmother of Chloe and Daniel Marshall.
 
Born: Bristol about 1860
 
Parents:  Richard Williams and Louisa Williams
 
Brother and Sister: Mary,  and Harold
 
Husband: James Daniel O'Connor
 
Children: Ethelina,   James,   Edward,   Robert,   Alfred Hartley,   Claude, and Joseph Somerville

Died: November 1900

Alice was the third child of Richard Williams, a Shopkeeper, and his wife Louisa , a Dressmaker. 

She was born in Bristol in about 1860, possibly in the area known as Christmas Steps.

She had at least one sister and possibly one brother. These were:-

Mary,  and Harold

She married James Daniel O'Connor and had 7 children. These were:-

Ethelina,   James,   Edward,   Robert,   Alfred Hartley,   Claude, and Joseph Somerville

It is not clear whether James Daniel was the father of Ethelina. It seems unlikely as Alice was only about 16 or 17 years old when Ethelina was born. Also in the 1881 Census Ethelina's name is given as "Ethelina W O'Connor", the "W" possibly standing for Williams.

The earliest reference to Alice is her Marriage Certificate for her marriage to James Daniel O'Connor on 10thApril 1880. When she married she was 19 years old and already had a 2 year old daughter Ethelina. Both she and James Daniel were living at 18 Alfred Cross Street, Leeds. This house appears to have been her mother's. Alice seems to have been unable to write as she signed the Marriage Register with an "X".

A year later when the  1881 Census was taken she, James Daniel and her two eldest children, Ethelina and James, were still living with her mother at 18 Alfred Cross Street, Leeds. Alice was 20 years old and worked as a Seamstress/Sewing Machine Hand, possibly for her mother who was a Dressmaker.

The next mention of Alice is the Birth Certificate of her son Alfred Hartley who was born in January 1888. Alice was now living at 16 Bywater Street, Leeds. Again when she signed the Birth Register she signed with an "X".

When the 1891 Census was taken Alice was living at 4 Bywater Street, Leeds with her husband and their 6 eldest children. She was now 31 years old.

Alice died from Breast Cancer in Leeds Infirmary on 3rd November 1900, aged 40.
