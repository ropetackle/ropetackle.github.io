Ethelina O'Connor In 1937

Relationship:  Ethelina O'Connor is a Great-Great-Grandmother of Chloe and Daniel Marshall
 
Born: 1878 in Leeds
 
Parents:  James Daniel O'Connor and Alice Louisa Williams
 
Brothers:  James,   Edward,   Robert,   Alfred Hartley,   Claude and Joseph Somerville
 
Husband:  William Davies
 
Children:  William Alfred (Bill) and Margaret Ethel (Peggy)
 
Died:  23rd December 1962

Ethelina O'Connor was born at Leeds, Yorkshire on the 3rd January 1878 and was the first child of Alice Louisa Williams.
Although she referred to James Daniel O'Connor as her father it is not certain that he was. When she was born her mother was only 16 or 17 years old  and did not marry James Daniel until 1880. It seems almost certain that Ethelina was illegitimate.
Confirmation of this comes from the fact that Ethelina always said that she had never had a Birth Certificate and a detailed search of the Records in the Births Registers for England and Wales at the Family Records Centre, London has so far failed to find any trace of her birth. It would thus appear that her birth was never registered. Also relevant is the fact that in the 1881 Census Her name is given as "Ethelina W O'Connor", the "W" probably standing for Williams.

She had six brothers. These were:-

James,   Edward,   Robert,   Alfred Hartley,   Claude and Joseph Somerville

The first reference to her is in the 1881 Census. She was then 3 years old and living with her mother, her "father", James Daniel O'Connor and brother James at the house of her maternal grandmother Louisa Williams at 18 Alfred Cross Street, Leeds. Her "father" was a Smith Forge Man and her mother a Seamstress/Sewing Machine Hand.

When her younger brother Alfred Hartley was born in 1888 the family were living at 16 Bywater Street, Leeds. James Daniel was now working as a Mill Labourer.

When the 1891 Census was taken the O'Connor family had moved to 4 Bywater Street. Ethelina was 13 years old and still at school. She now had 5 brothers, James, Edward, Robert, Alfred Hartley and Claude. James Daniel was working as a Mechanical Engineer.

By the time that the 1901 Census was taken there had been a dramatic change in her family circumstances. Her mother had died of Breast Cancer in November 1900 and she and her youngest brother Joseph, who was only 1 year old, were living with her Grandmother Louisa Williams at 15 Scarsdale Street, Leeds. Although she was now 23 years old she was not working.
Her "father" James Daniel, who now worked as a Joiner, lived nearby, still at 4 Bywater Street with three of her brothers, Robert, Alfred and Claude.

According to her daughter, Peggy Davies, Ethelina was not brought up to work, she was “brought up to be a lady”, and it was intended that she would make a good marriage. This is born out by the fact that although she was 23 when the 1901 Census was taken, she had no occupation.

Some time after 1901, Ethelina and her Grandmother, Louisa Williams, moved to Liverpool to live with Ethelina's Aunt Mary, Louisa's daughter. They lived somewhere near the centre of Liverpool, in the area between Mount Pleasant and Lime Street. Ethelina's daughter remembers, when she was young, going into Liverpool on the tram from Prescott and her mother pointing out a house and saying that it was there that she had been brought up.

Ethelina was a superb needle-woman, having apparently learnt the skills when a child from the girls who worked in her grandmother’s dressmaking workshop.

Again, according to Peggy Davies, Ethelina's Aunt Mary was well off and had many wealthy friends amongst whom were the Costain family who founded the building firm of that name. Both families were pushing for Ethelina to marry one of the Costain sons but she did not want to do this and instead went to train as a nurse to escape the pressure. At that time nursing was considered to be an acceptable career for a "lady" as it involved a course of training.

At some time she started working at the Rainhill Mental Asylum as a nurse. It was while she was working there that she met her future husband William Davies.

Ethelina married William Davies on the 28th April 1909, aged 30, at Christ Church, the Parish Church of Eccleston, near St. Helens, Lancashire. When she married she gave the name of James Daniel O'Connor as her father.
