Parents:  Richard Williams and Louisa Williams
Brother and Sister:  Harold and Alice Louisa
 

Mary Williams was the daughter of Richard Williams, a Shopkeeper, and his wife Louisa, a Dressmaker. She is thought to have been born in Bristol, Gloucestershire, possibly in the part of the city known as Christmas Steps.

She is thought to have had at least one brother and one sister. These were:-

Harold and Alice Louisa

According to her great-niece, Peggy Davies, Mary married either a German or someone with a Germanic sounding name and they ran a Guesthouse in Liverpool, somewhere in the area between Mount Pleasant and Lime Street. She remembers her mother, Ethelina O'Connor, once pointing out a tall multi-storey house as they were travelling on the tram into Liverpool and being told that it was there that she, i.e. Ethelina, had been brought up by her Aunt Mary.

At some time after 1901 Mary's mother, Louisa Williams, and Mary's niece Ethelina O'Connor, Alice’s daughter and eldest child, moved from Leeds to Liverpool to live with Mary.

Mary and her husband seem to have been fairly well off and apparently had many wealthy friends including the Costain family. These were wealthy builders who founded the large building group, Costains.

In her old age Mary Williams became ill and lived with Ethelina O'Connor and Ethelina’s husband William Davies at Palatine Cottages, Rainhill Road, Nut Grove, St Helens, Lancashire. However when Ethelina was expecting her second child she found that she could not cope with the ailing Mary, a young son and pregnancy. Mary therefore went into Whiston Hospital - which was the former workhouse and therefore very much looked upon by old people as a place of destitution - where she died just before Peggy Davies was born in 1913.
