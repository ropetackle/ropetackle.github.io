Helen and Robin Marshall were married at Romford Register Office on the 4th July 1964.

Because the cost of housing in the London area was so high Helen and Robin could not afford to buy a house in the South East. Robin therefore decided to leave May & Bakers and look for a job in a part of the country where housing was cheaper. He joined an American Company, Engelhards, who manufactured precious metal chemical compounds. Engelhards had recently moved their factory to Cinderford, Gloucestershire which is in the Forest of Dean. There they bought a small bungalow, “Maranatha”, Woodside Avenue, Cinderford.

On the 26th August 1965 Helen's second child Ian David was born.

In August 1966 Robin left Engelhard's and joined the Government's Factory Inspectorate in Swindon. The family moved to the village of Cricklade in Wiltshire where they bought a house, 27 Pauls Croft, on a new estate.

In 1970 Robin was posted to the Factory Inspectorate's Office in Huddersfield, Yorkshire and Helen and the family moved to 62 Ben Rhydding Road, Ilkley in 1971.
