James and Elizabeth Ann Smith married at Helston Parish Church on 5th September 1901.
The Marriage Certificate is interesting. When James married he was only 19 and not of "full age". In England in those days you could not legally marry without your parent's consent if you were under 21. James clearly didn't want to try and get parental permission and therefore lied about his age, giving his age as 22. Also on the Marriage Certificate, the space for his father's name is blank.

It isn't known whether James and Elizabeth remained in Helston after they married or, if they did, how long they stayed there.

However when their son Edward John was born on 22nd September 1906, James and Elizabeth had moved to Plymouth. They were living at 25 Holdsworth Street in an area known as Pennycomequick. this lies just to the North of the Railway Station and the house would have been very convenient for him to get to work. James was still employed as a Fireman on the Great Western Railway.

In 1907 James moved from Plymouth to Princetown on Dartmoor.

Although there is now no station at Princetown, the house they lived in is still there. It was one of a terrace of three houses, now known as the Old Station Cottages, in Station Road. The railway from Plymouth to Princetown had been opened in 1883 and the Cottages and neighbouring Station Master's house had been built by the Great Western Railway at about the same time.

James and his family lived in Princetown for 2 or 3 years and then, some time before 1910 or 1911, they moved to Newton Abbott, Devon where they lived at 4 Powderham Terrace. James was still living at Powderham Terrace when Elizabeth Ann died in 1929.

Newton Abbott was an important station on the main Great Western Railway from Cornwall to London. Whether James moved there on promotion to Engine Driver isn't known but it seems possible. However he did become a driver and eventual was a driver on the Great Western Railway's most prestigious train, the Cornish Riviera Express, which ran to London.

After Elizabeth Ann died James married his cousin Lillie.

When James retired from the railway he went to live in Bristol. He was living in Bristol, at 454 Fishponds Road, when he died on 30th April 1952 aged 70. The cause of death was Heart Failure.

A more detailed account of the family background of James Kingdon, his life and his personality is given in the extracts from the diary of his son, Edward John Kingdon.
