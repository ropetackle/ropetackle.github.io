James Kingdon

Relationship: James Kingdon is a Great-Great-Grandfather of Faye, Jenny and Kate Lloyd and of Chloe and Daniel Marshall
 
Born: 1881 at South Molton, Devon
 
Mother: Susanna Kingdon (father unknown)

Died: 1952

James Kingdon was born in South Molton, Devon in August 1881.  He was illegitimate and was the first child of Susanna Kingdon, a General Servant. His father is unknown.

In 1883 his mother married John Ford, a Gardener , but James never took his stepfather’s name, keeping his mother’s maiden name of Kingdon.

James had at least 3 stepbrothers and 8 stepsisters. These were:-

Robert, Sarah, Ruth, Esther, Elizabeth, Gertrude, Eva, Jessie, Daisy, Fredrick, and William

When James was born it is thought that his Mother and Grandfather were living at 2 Bennetts Court, South Molton as that is where they were living when the 1881 Census was taken. Bennetts Court may have been in East Street.

After his mother's marriage she and her husband  lived for a time at Romansleigh, Devon but by 1885 or 1886 they had moved to Morchard Bishop, Devon.

The earliest reference to James is in the 1891 Census. He was 9 years old and living with his mother and stepfather and their four eldest children in the Parish of Morchard Bishop where his stepfather was working as an Agricultural Labourer. The Census gives their address as Watcombe. This could have been a farm near the hamlet of Knightstone or Farthing Park which are about 2 kilometers east of Morchard Bishop. He is described as a Scholar which would indicate that he was still at school.

In late 1891 or 1892 the family moved to the Parish of Colebrooke, Devon. It is almost certain that they were living in  Copplestone, which is in the Parish of Colebrooke. What is known is that, although James had left home, the rest of the family were living at 1 Furzedown Cottages, Copplestone when the 1901 Census was taken.

James left school to start work as a Farm Labourer "well before I was ten". As his birthday was in August this probably means that he left school at Easter 1882 - Easter being the start of the busy season on a farm.

James could clearly see that there was no future in being a Farm Labourer and when he was16 he left home and joined the Great Western Railway at Exeter as a Cleaner.
A Cleaner was the lowest grade of footplate man on the railways. His main job was to clean the engine, rake out the firebox, i.e. remove the ashes, and prepare the engine, for example lighting the fire, ready for the fireman and driver when they reported for duty. Although it sounds like a menial job it was in fact the first stage of an apprenticeship which led to person becoming first a Locomotive Fireman and finally a Driver. In those days all Engine Drivers started their careers as Cleaners.

From Exeter James moved to Helston where he lodged with Elizabeth Ann Smith's family in St John's Road, Helston. He was living with them when the 1901 Census was taken. It seems most likely that he moved to Helston on promotion to Fireman, as that is the occupation he gives on the Census Return. The Census gives his age as 19.

Later that year James and Elizabeth Ann married.

A more detailed account of the family background of James Kingdon and his life is given in the extracts from the diary of his son, Edward John Kingdon.
