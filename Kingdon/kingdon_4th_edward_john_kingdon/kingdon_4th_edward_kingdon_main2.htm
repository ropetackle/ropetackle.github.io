Edward John Kingdon and Marjorie Tate were married at Romford Registry Office on 5th July 1930 he and set up home at 129 Mill Lane, in Chadwell Heath, Essex.

It was while they were living in Mill Lane that Jack's only child, Helen Marjorie, was born on 3rd March 1936.

During the war Jack continued to work as a teacher.

After the war Jack and his family moved to 14 Kings Avenue, Chadwell Heath, Essex.

Jack suffered badly from Angina during his last few years of teaching and eventually he was forced to take early retirement on medical grounds.

Jack had always had a desire to go back to Devon and in 1965 he and Marjorie moved to Exmouth. This was not a success and he and Marjorie again moved, this time to 5 Gorse Meadow, in Bourton On The Water, Gloucestershire. This was reasonably near to Cricklade, Wiltshire, where their daughter Helen and her family were living. 

In August 1981 they left Bourton and moved 39 Skipton Road, Ilkley, so as to be near Helen.

Jack lived in Ilkley until his death. He died on 1st November 1982 at Otley General Hospital. the cause of his death was Myocardial Infraction.

During the period from October 1978 to October 1982 he wrote a diary. As well as covering everyday events at Bourton on the Water and at Ilkley, the diary also contains many reminiscences of his childhood and his parents as well other interesting memories of people and events such as the wedding of his granddaughter Jane in 1981.
