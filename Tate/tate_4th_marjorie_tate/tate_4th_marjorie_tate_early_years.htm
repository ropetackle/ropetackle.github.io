Diary of Edward John Kingdon
Marjorie Tate was born on the 29th January 1905 at 38 Bryn Glas, Pen-y-darren, Merthyr Tydfil, South Wales. She was the sixth child of David Tate, who was the Golf Professional at Morlais Castle Golf Club, and Frances Martha Clio Roberts.

When Marjorie was born her parents already had two daughters, Florence and Dorothy Alma, and three sons, John, Robert Frederick and Edward Reginald.

Condition in their house at 38 Bryn Glas, Pen-y-darren, Merthyr, which only had two bedrooms, must have been crowded with six and later seven or eight children in it. An indication of how crowded can be gained from the entry for the 27th April 1981 in the diary of her husband Edward John Kingdon.

"Woke at 6.30 am. to find electricity restored, no more snow fallen but the wind still strong and bitterly cold. Reminisced a lot about Helen and the children as we sat by the fire and also our own childhood. Mum gave a lovely, charming, jolly and unselfconscious laugh when the word hypothermia recalled to her the time when there were not less than three sleeping to a bed and she sometimes found herself on the floor, heaved there by sister Dolly's not inconsiderable bum. Her face took on a lovely expression and seemed quite young and girlish in the fire light. We felt very close."
