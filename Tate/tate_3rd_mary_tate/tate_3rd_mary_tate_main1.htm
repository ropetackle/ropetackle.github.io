Description of Rickerby

Mary Tate was born sometime between 1862 and 1865 in Longtown, Cumberland. She was the fifth child of George Tate and Elizabeth Huggon.

Mary had seven brothers and two sisters. These were:-

  • Thomas
  • Betsy
  • James
  • George
  • Edward
  • David
  • John
  • Sarah
  • Robert

Mary Tate was born in Longtown, which is on the A7, about 6 miles north of Carlisle, and near the Scottish Border.
It is not clear when she was born. According to the 1871 and 1881 Census Returns she was born in 1862 or 1863. However the age given for her in the 1891 Census would mean that she was born in 1864 or 1865. It is most likely that she was born in 1862 or 1863.

Some time between about 1863 and 1866 the Tate family moved from Longtown to the small Estate village of Rickerby, in the Parish of Stanwix.

Rickerby is on the north bank of the River Eden, about 1/2 mile east of Carlisle and just off the A689. The village "belonged" to the MacInnes family who lived at Rickerby House.
Descriptions of Rickerby, based on the 1881 and 1891 Census Returns and other documents, are given elsewhere in this family history.

The earliest reference to Mary is in the 1871 Census. She was 8 years old and living with her parents in Rickerby.

The next mention of Mary is in the 1881 Census. She was 18 years old, working as a Vest Maker (Tailor) and living with her parents in Rickerby.

Ten years later, when the 1891 Census was taken, Mary was still single and living with her parents at Rickerby. She was now working as a Machinist, presumably in the tailoring trade. Interestingly she seems to have "lost" two years off her age as she is described on the Census Return as being 26, not 28.
