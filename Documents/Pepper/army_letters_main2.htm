Letter 2 - 10th December 1910

Infantry Record Office,
Hamilton, N.B.
10th December 1910.

Madam,

Please note that a passage to Durban, South Africa, has been allocated you on board the transport “Soudan” embarking at Southampton on 20th December 1910 between 11 a.m. and 6 p.m.

You are allowed conveyance for 2 cwts [101.6 kg.] of baggage. You should take with you a box not higher than 14 inches marked with the name of your intended husband, his rank and corps, and labelled both ends with your name, and the name, rank and corps of your intended husband, and Durban, the port at which you will dis-embark in South Africa. A supply of labels is enclosed.

Under later arrangements, you will only be required to pay your travelling expenses to the port of embarkation, Southampton. You will receive food free of cost on board ship and your travelling expenses in South Africa will be defrayed.

Yours faithfully,
L H Caird, Major
for Colonel i/c Records No. 2 District.

Miss F.Pepper
12 Newthorpe St.
Nottingham.
