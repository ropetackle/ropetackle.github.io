Letter 1 - 20th July 1901

Infantry Record Office,
Hamilton, Scotland.
20th July 1910.

Madam,

An application has been received from the Officer Commanding 1st Battalion Royal Scots Fusiliers, for an indulgence passage for you to proceed to South Africa for the purpose of being married to No.6877 Sergeant T. Marshall.

If a passage is granted, you would probably require to proceed next Trooping Season, that is, between the months of September 1910 and March 1911.

You would require to pay your own expenses to the Port of Embarkation, probably Southampton, and also, if necessary, from the port of Disembarkation to destination in South Africa.

You would require to pay for your food on board ship, approximately 21 days at 1/- per diem. [5p. a day]

Please inform me if you are prepared to accept a passage under these conditions.

Yours faithfully,
L H Caird, Major
For Colonel i/c Records No.2 District.

Miss F.Pepper,
12 Newthorpe St,
off Arkwright St,
Nottingham.
