April 4th 1980

Last night watched a very interesting programme on dyslexia; the probable reason for my mother’s inability to read or write. Made several attempts to teach her when I was between the ages of 12 and 15, but she seemed to be unable to recognise the letters even, the lesson usually ending in tears, distressing for both of us.

My mother never spoke of her early days and I never asked any questions. That she had had small pox, was a delicate child, that her father was a Master Tailor – very proud of this – keeping a shop in Coinage Hall Street?, Helston, and that she had two sisters Janie – Olive’s mother, and Katey the youngest – Violet’s mother, and a brother Jack who was drowned at sea is about the total of my factual knowledge. Jack’s photo in what I assume to be a midshipman’s uniform and looking very young was always on display in our sitting room, in which we rarely sat. I assume it was because her brother was called Jack that she always called me so, although I was christened John. I was curious about this when young but never asked why.
