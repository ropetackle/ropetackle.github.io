Origin of the Kingdon Surname

According to the Oxford Dictionary of Surnames the origin of the surname Kingdon is:-

"English (Devon): Habitation name from Higher Kingdon in Alverdiscott, Devon or from Kendon in North Bovey, Devon. Both are from the Old English cyning KING + dūn HILL. A variation of the name is Kindon."

The former is the most likely as Alverdiscott (Grid Ref. SS520252) lies part way between Bideford and South Molton, about 12 miles West of South Molton.

It should be borne in mind that when looking at C19th.  and earlier records and registers that the various officials, e.g. Census Enumerators, Clergy, Registrars Of Births, Marriages and Deaths, often recorded the name "Kingdon" incorrectly. They used the more commonly encountered name "Kingdom".

The Distribution of Kingdon Families in Devon

A Study of the 1999 Telephone Directories covering Devon gave 165 entries for the Kingdon name. Of these 40 (24%) lived within about 5 miles of South Molton and of these 33 (20% of the total number in Devon) were in South Molton itself.
