“…well my dear you mentioned about our Grand parents, Im like you don’t know anything of their history, only that Granma Smith lived with Auntie Katie, & a tall boney woman, Violet & I used to tease her & she would try & catch us, to give us a hiding with her stick kid like, and I do remember the day of her funeral, we were both dresses up in dead black from head to foot, and were like Queens, on having a ride in the horse & carriage to her funeral, I said to Vi, Im going to be a good girl now a’rnt going to swear no more, expect it was all forgotten by time we returned from the ride, I think I was about 7 then, did not know any more of mother’s side, more than Ive heard mother say, that grandfather Smith was a Tailor & head man up Basil Thomas outfitters shop here in Helston, and Auntie Katie the Trousers hand, Auntie Bessie coat hand & mother waist-coat hand, Jack, one Brother was in the Navy P.O. and their youngest brother was in the Foundry at Falmouth, died young, he was buried at Helston, also Gradad & Granma Smith, they lived at Falmouth when the children were young, as Ive heard mother say they lived on the Terrace above “Jacob’s Ladder” and used to slide on their bellys down the Ladder, & mother remembed when they were kepted indoors when the Soldier’s from the Castle came in & rioted the town, broke shop windows etc, as some of their mates were in Falmouth Jail for being drunk & disorderly, but regrets I haven’t any Photo’s of them….”
