When Florence's husband Thomas left the Army in 1922 it was not a particularly good time to be looking for employment, especially in Glasgow. It was the height of the Depression and the level of unemployment was high. He and Florence had thought of getting work as the Steward and Stewardess at a club somewhere but this did not come about.

The only employment that Thomas seems to have found was that of a Commissionaire under the auspices of the Royal Corps Of Commissionaires. This organisation found work for former Non Commissioned Officers as Doormen, Receptionists and Night Watchmen at Offices and other commercial premises. It was not well paid and Florence had to go out to work. What she did is not known although she certainly worked for a period as a cook in a canteen.

When her son married in 1937, Thomas and Florence were living in Hillington, Glasgow at 83 Bearford Drive and Thomas was working as a Watchman.

After Thomas died in 1941 Florence continued to live in Glasgow. However when her son and his family moved to 203 Dentons Green Lane, St Helens, she went to live with them.

This arrangement was not satisfactory and eventually, in the early 1960s, it became so unbearable for Tommy's wife Peggy that Florence went to live at an Old People's Residential Home at Moss Bank, St Helens. She was living there when she died on 19th August 1966 aged 87. Her Death Certificate gave the cause of her death as Myocardial Failure, Arteriosclerosis and Senility.
