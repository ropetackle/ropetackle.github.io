Florence Pepper in the 1930s

Relationship: Florence Pepper is a Great-Great-Grandmother of Chloe and Daniel Marshall.
 
Born: 1879 in Nottingham
 
Parents: Robert Pepper and Ann Hodges
 
Brothers and Sisters: Alice,  Eliza,  Robert
 
Husband: Thomas Marshall
 
Children: Thomas Robert (Tommy)
 
Died: 1966

Florence Pepper was born on the 23rd January 1879 in Nottingham, at 25 Newbridge Street, Meadows. She was the third child of Robert Pepper, a Railway Labourer, and Ann Hodges.

Florence had two sisters and one brother. These were:-

Alice Mary, Eliza and Robert John Henry.

The earliest reference to Florence is her Birth Certificate. When she was born her father was about 40 years old and worked as a Labourer in the Provinder Stores of the Midland Railway Company. Her mother was about 33.
When she was born her parents already had two children, Alice Mary and Eliza.

The next mention of Florence is in the 1881 Census. She was 2 years old and her family were still living at 25 Newbridge Street. Her father was aged 42 and described as a Railway Labourer while her mother was 35 and worked as a Maker Up at a Hosiery Works.

Ten years later, when the 1891 Census was taken there had been considerable changes in the family circumstances. The family were still living at 25 Newbridge Street but there was now a younger brother, Robert John Henry, who was only 9 years old. More importantly her father had died in December 1882.
Florence was only 12 years old but had already left school to earn a living and was working as an Errand Girl to help support the family.

Life must have been hard for her mother and the family without the income of Florence's father, particularly as her second sister, Eliza, who was now 16, was, according to the Census Return still at school.
According to Florence's daughter-in-law, Peggy Davies, Florence had told her that Eliza had suffered some form of brain damage as a child and was not capable of earning a living.
The only income the family had was that which her mother Annie and eldest sister Alice earned from their work as Hosiery Menders and that which Florence brought in as 12 year old Errand Girl. None of this work would have been well paid.

When the 1901 Census was taken there had been further changes in the family. Florence's mother seems to have died. The Census shows that Alice had married a John Thomas Prior, a Self-Employed Undertaker and Joiner, and Florence, Eliza and Robert were living with them at 12 Newthorpe Street, Nottingham. Florence was 22 years old and working as a Hosiery Mender.

It was while she was living there that, in 1909, she met Sergeant Thomas Marshall of the 1st Battalion, Royal Scots Fusiliers who had arrived home on leave from India in March of that year and was staying nearby with his parents or relatives.

By the time Thomas and the Battalion returned to overseas service in the October of 1909 he and Florence were engaged.
