Born: About 1836 or 1837
 
Parents: Thomas Pepper and Alice Sharp
 
Brothers and Sisters:  Fanny,  Robert,  Elizabeth,  Esther,  Thomas,  Jane

William Pepper was born in Leicestershire, probably in Swepstone, in about 1836 or 1837. He was the second child of Thomas Pepper, an Agricultural Labourer, and Alice Sharp.

William had at least two brothers and four sisters. These were:-

Fanny, Robert, Elizabeth, Esther, Thomas and Jane

When William's younger brother Robert was born in February 1839 he was living with his family at Newton Nethercote, in the Parish of Swepstone, Leicestershire.
The current Ordinance Survey map for Swepstone does not show any place called Newton Nethercote. There is a village called Newton Burgoland and a Nethercote Farm. Both of these are in the parish of Swepstone and about 1 km away. As his father was an Agricultural Labourer it is possible that they were actually living at Nethercote Farm.

The only reference to William is in the 1841 Census. He was then 4 years old and living with his parents at Dishley, in the Parish of Swepstone.
Dishley Farm is about 1km. from Swepstone and it is assumed that her father was working at that farm at the time.

When his sister Esther was born in February 1843 William and his family were living in Swepstone.

By the time the 1851 Census was taken he was no longer living at home.
