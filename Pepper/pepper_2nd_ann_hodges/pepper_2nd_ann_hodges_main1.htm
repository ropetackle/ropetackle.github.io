Relationship: Ann Hodges is a Great-Great-Great-Grandmother of Chloe and Daniel Marshall.
 
Born: 22nd June 1846 in Basford, Nottingham
 
Parents: Morrisson Hodges and Ann Crump
 
Husband: Robert Pepper
 
Children: Alice,  Eliza,  Florence and Robert
 
Died: Between 1891 and 1901

Ann Hodges was born in Nottingham, at Basford, on 22nd June 1846. She was the daughter of Morrisson Hodges, a Needle Maker, and Ann Crump.

Ann married Robert Pepper, a Labourer, and they had 4 children. These were:-

Alice, Eliza, Florence and Robert

The first reference to Ann is on her Birth Certificate. When he registered her birth her father gave his occupation as that of a Needle Maker.

The next mentioned of Ann is when she married Robert Pepper in Nottingham on 8 th October 1870 at the Parish Church of the Parish of St John Baptist. The Marriage Certificate states that she was 24 years old and living in High Street, Nottingham. Incidentally it gives her name as Anna

When her daughter Florence was born in January 1879, Ann, or Annie as she now seems to be called, was living at 25 Newbridge Street, Meadows, Nottingham and she and Robert already had 2 children, Alice and Eliza.

Annie is next mentioned in the 1881 Census. She was now 35 years old  and still living at 25 Newbridge Street. She worked as a Hosiery Works Maker Up.

The next 18 months after the Census were quite dramatic for Annie. At some time in the 12 months after the Census her son Robert was born and on 24th December 1882 her husband Robert died leaving her to bring up the family on her own.

When the 1891 Census was taken Annie gave her age as 43. She was still living at 25 Newbridge Street. To support herself and her family she worked as a Hosiery Mender. Hosiery Mending, which would be done for a Hosiery manufacturer, involved making good defects in new hosiery and was typically done at home and was "piece work" and therefore poorly paid. She probably worked at home with her eldest daughter Alice who was also a Hosiery Mender and still lived with her.

Life must have been extremely hard for Annie and her family without her husband's income. Her son Robert was only 9 and still at school. What must have made conditions harder was that her second daughter Eliza, who was now 16, is believed to have suffered from some form of brain damage and was not capable of earning a living and thereby adding to the family income. Their only income, apart from that which she and Alice earned by their Hosiery Mending, was that which Florence could get as 12 year old Errand Girl.

Annie is thought to have died between Census of 1891 and that of 1901 as the latter shows that Eliza, Florence and Robert were then living with their older married sister Alice and her husband.
