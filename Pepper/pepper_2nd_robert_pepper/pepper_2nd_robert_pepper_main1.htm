Relationship: Robert Pepper is a Great-Great-Great-Grandfather of Chloe and Daniel Marshall.

Born: 1839 at Swepstone, Leicestershire
 
Parents: Thomas Pepper and Alice Sharp
 
Brothers and Sisters: Fanny,  William,  Elizabeth,  Esther,  Thomas,  Jane
 
Wife: Ann Hodges
 
Children: Alice,  Eliza,  Florence,  Robert
 
Died: 1882

Robert Pepper was born at Newton Nethercote, Swepstone, Leicestershire on 16th February 1839. He was the third child of Thomas Pepper, an Agricultural Labourer, and his wife Alice Sharp.

He had at least two brothers and four sisters. These were:-

Fanny, William, Elizabeth, Esther, Thomas and Jane

Robert married Ann Hodges and they had 4 children. These were:-

Alice, Eliza, Florence and Robert

The first reference to Robert is on his Birth Certificate. He was born on 16th February 1839 at Newton Nethercote, Swepstone. His father's occupation is given as an Agricultural Labourer. When his mother registered his birth she did not sign but instead "made her mark" with an X.
The current Ordinance Survey map for Swepstone does not show any place called Newton Nethercote. There is a village called Newton Burgoland and a Nethercote Farm. Both of theses are in the parish of Swepstone and about 1 km away from the village. As his father was an Agricultural Labourer it is possible that they were actually living at Nethercote Farm.


Robert is next mentioned in the 1841 Census. He was now 2 years old and living with his parents at Dishley, Swepstone, Leicestershire. His father was still an Agricultural Labourer.
Dishley Farm is about 1km. from Swepstone and it is assumed that his father was working at that farm at the time.

When Robert's sister Esther was born in February 1843 the family were living in Swepstone itself and his father still worked as an Agricultural labourer.

We next hear of Robert in the 1851 Census. Robert was by then 12 years old and at school and his family were still living in Swepstone village and his father still worked as an Agricultural Labourer..

On 8th October 1870 Robert married Ann Hodges at the Parish Church in the Parish of St John Baptist in Nottingham. When he married Ann he was 31 years old and working as a Labourer. He gave his residence as Canal Street.

Robert is next mentioned in January 1879 when his daughter Florence was born. He was working as a Labourer in the Provinder Stores of the Midland Railway Company and living at 25 Newbridge Street, Meadows, Nottingham.

Robert was still living at 25 Newbridge Street when the 1881 Census was taken. This gives Robert's age as 42 and his occupation as that of Railway Labourer.

At some time in the next 12 months his son Robert was born.

On 24th December 1882, only a few months after his son was born, Robert died. He was still young, only 43 years old. The cause of his death was pneumonia, gangrene of the lung and exhaustion.
