Born: About 1834 or 1835
 
Parents: Thomas Pepper and Alice Sharp
 
Brothers and Sisters:  William,  Robert,  Elizabeth,  Esther,  Thomas,  Jane

Fanny Pepper was born in Leicestershire, probably in Swepstone, in about 1834 or 1835. She was the eldest child of Thomas Pepper, an Agricultural Labourer, and Alice Sharp.

Fanny had at least three brothers and three sisters. These were:-

William, Robert, Elizabeth, Esther, Thomas and Jane

When Fanny's younger brother Robert was born in February 1839 she was living with her family at Newton Nethercote, in the Parish of Swepstone, Leicestershire.
The current Ordinance Survey map for Swepstone does not show any place called Newton Nethercote. There is a village called Newton Burgoland and a Nethercote Farm. Both of these are in the parish of Swepstone and about 1 km away. As her father was an Agricultural Labourer it is possible that they were actually living at Nethercote Farm.

The only reference to Fanny is in the 1841 Census. She was then 6 years old and living with her parents at Dishley, in the Parish of Swepstone.
Dishley Farm is about 1km. from Swepstone and it is assumed that her father was working at that farm at the time.

When her sister Esther was born in February 1843 Fanny and her family were living in Swepstone.

By the time the 1851 Census was taken she was no longer living at home.
