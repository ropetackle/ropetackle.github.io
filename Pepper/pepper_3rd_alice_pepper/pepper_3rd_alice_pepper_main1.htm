Born: About 1871 or 1872 in Nottingham
 
Parents: Robert Pepper and Ann Hodges
 
Brothers and Sisters: Eliza,  Florence,  Robert
 
Husband: John Prior

Alice Mary Pepper was born in about 1871 or 1872 in Nottingham. She was the first child of Robert Pepper, a Railway Labourer, and Ann Hodges.

Alice had two sisters and one brother. These were:-

Eliza, Florence and Robert John Henry

The first reference to Alice is the 1881 Census. She was 9 years old and living with her parents at 25 Newbridge Street, Meadows, Nottingham. Her father worked as a Railway Labourer and her mother as a Hosiery Works Maker Up.

In December 1882 her father died and her mother was left to bring up the three children, the youngest of whom was about 1 year old, on her own.

When the 1891 Census was taken Alice was 19 years old and still living at home in Newbridge Street. Like her mother she worked as Hosiery Mender. This work, which would be done for a Hosiery Manufacturer, would involve making good defects in new hosiery and was typically done at home and was therefore not well paid. Probably Alice and her mother worked at home together.

By the time of the 1901 Census Alice, who was now 29 years old, had married John Thomas Prior, a self-employed Joiner and Undertaker and they were living at 12 Newthorpe Street, Nottingham. Her mother seems to have died as her sisters and brother were also living with her and her husband.
