I love this photograph of, left to right, Uncle Jack, Mum, Uncle Vic and Aunty Dolly, probably taken about 1917. They all look so well and happy and smoothly turned out. You can see that they all had beautiful hair - Uncle Jack's and Mum's was dark brown, Vic and Dolly had auburn locks. Aunty Dolly was very proud of her hair - she has tied it up in a huge bow, the height of fashion then. I expect Mum's frock was made by Aunty Florrie, the oldest child, who made most of the younger children's clothes. Vic has an Eton Collar, also very fashionable. This photo was framed in the parlour at 38 Brynglas St, where I loved to study it (Helen Kingdon)
