This photograph, taken in 1933, shows my grandad (David Tate) on the left ( grandma in front) with his brother Ned and wife and friend.
Grandad had become a very calm and kindly man in his old age although he had been fiery-tempered in younger life. We grandchildren looked forward to his ‘bristley kiss’ and being taken to help on his allotment where he would let us eat young peas and carrots. My picture of him sitting in his wooden arm-chair next to the range, reading his paper, at peace with the world, is very different from my mother’s recollections of an irascible father! (Helen  Kingdon)
