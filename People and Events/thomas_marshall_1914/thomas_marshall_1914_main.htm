this photograph shows my Grandpa Marshall with his Platoon in South Africa in 1914. He is the Sergeant in the centre of the photograph 
The inscription on the photograph says " No.5 Platoon R. S. Fusiliers. Winners of the Murray Cup, Pretoria, 1914"

I wonder how many of the men shown here survived the horrors of World War I - they could not have dreamt what they would be facing in a few months time.
Between August 1914 and January 1915 the Battalion lost 261 men out of 1000, a quarter of its strength. This means that 7 of these 28 men may not have survived the first 5 months of the War. (Robin Marshall)
