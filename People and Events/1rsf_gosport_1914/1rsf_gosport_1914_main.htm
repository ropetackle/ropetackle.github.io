This photograph, which was probably taken from a local newspaper, shows the 1st Battalion, Royal Scots Fusiliers leaving for France in August 1914. It was possibly taken on the 14th of that month - the day that they sailed for France as part of the British Expeditionary Force. They were not to return to Britain until 1919. Somewhere on this photograph must be my Grandpa Marshall.
It is poignant to think that when the Battalion left they numbered about 1,000 men. By January 1915 they are estimated to have had 261 fatal casualties as well as numerous men injured. What a waste of lives in a pointless cause. (Robin Marshall)
