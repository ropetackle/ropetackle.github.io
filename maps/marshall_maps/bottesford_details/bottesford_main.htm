Easthorpe, Leicestershire, (Grid Ref. SK810384), is where William Marshall and Mary Lamb lived and where their son, Thomas Marshall, was born in 1850. He was baptized at Bottesford Parish Church
Although modern day Easthorpe is a small hamlet outside Bottesford it appears that, in the 19th Century, Easthorpe was all of the area of Bottesford south of the River Devon. The Church was therefore in Bottesford but Church Street was in Easthorpe. This would explain why the 19th Century Census Returns for Easthorpe show that its population was much larger that its present size would indicate.

When the 1841 Census was taken, Edward Lamb, who was a Publican, his wife Ann Lamb and daughter Mary Lamb were living in Church Street, Easthorpe, Bottesford. 
Twenty years later, when the 1861 Census was taken, Mary Lamb, her husband William Marshall, their five children and Mary's mother, Ann Lamb, were living at The Beershop, Easthorpe, Bottesford. It is possibly that they were living at the premises where her father had been the Publican before he died.

Although no further information is given as to their addresses, it is possible that on one or other of these occasions they were living at the Public House which is now known as the Red Lion and which is on the corner of Church Street and Grantham Road. (Robin Marshall)
