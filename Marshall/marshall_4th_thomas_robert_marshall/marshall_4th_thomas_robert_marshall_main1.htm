Tommy Marshall In 1934

Relationship: Thomas Robert Marshall is a Great-Grandfather of Chloe and Daniel Marshall
 
Born: 13th October 1911 in Transvaal, South Africa
 
Parents: Thomas Marshall and Florence Pepper
 
Wife: Margaret Ethel Davies (Peggy)
 
Children: Robin,  Alan,  Brian
 
Died: 14th July 1970

Thomas Robert Marshall (known as Tommy) was the only child of Thomas Marshall, a Sergeant in the 1st Battalion Royal Scots Fusiliers, and Florence Pepper. He was born on 13th October 1911 at Roberts Heights, Pretoria, in the Province of Transvaal,  South Africa and was Baptised at the Garrison Church at Roberts Heights on 26th November 1911.

On the 19th February 1914, with the increasing threat of a war against Germany, Tommy and his parents returned to Britain when the 1 Battalion were recalled from South Africa. They arrived at Gosport, Hampshire, a month later.

When his father was discharged from the Army having completed his 21 years service they were living in Glasgow.

Tommy won a scholarship to Allen Glens Grammar School in Glasgow and wanted to become a Civil Engineer. Unfortunately his parents could not afford for him to stay at school and go to University so he joined Triplex Northern, a subsidiary of the glass makers Pilkington Brothers, as an Office Boy.
Triplex Northern supplied safety glass for vehicle windscreens and he eventually became their Commercial Representative for Scotland, dealing with the bus operators in that Country.

When he was in Glasgow he was a keen cyclist and when he came to St Helens, where Head Office of the Pilkington Group was to train as their Commercial Representative, he joined the St Helens Section of the Cyclist Touring Club. It was through this interest that he met his future wife, Peggy Davies.

Tommy and Peggy became engaged and they married at Rainhill Parish Church, St Helens on 19th June 1937
