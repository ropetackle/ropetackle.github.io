Relationship: Mary Lamb is a Great-Great-Great-Great-Grandmother of Chloe and Daniel Marshall
 
Born: About 1824 or 1825 in Bottesford, Leicestershire
 
Parents: Thomas Lamb and Ann Lamb
 
Husband: William Marshall
 
Children: Thomas,  William,  John,  George,  Ellen
 

Mary was the daughter of Thomas Lamb, a Publican, and his wife Ann. She was born at Easthorpe, Bottesford, Leicestershire in 1824 or 1825.

She married William Marshall, a Bricklayer, in July 1850 and they had at least 5 children. These were:-
Thomas,  William,  John,  George,  and Ellen.

The first reference to Mary seems to be in the 1841 Census. An Edward Lamb, a Publican aged 50, was living in Church Street, Easthorpe, Bottesford with his wife Ann Lamb, aged 40, and his daughter Mary, aged 15. Although no further information is given as to their address, it is possible that they were living at the Public House which is now known as the Red Lion and which is on the corner of Church Street and Grantham Road.

Although the Census gives the forename of the Head of the Household as Edward, not Thomas, the age of his wife and daughter, together with his trade of Publican, tally with the information from Mary Lamb's Marriage Certificate for 1850 and the 1861 Census. It seems likely that the two Mary Lambs were the one and the same person.

Although modern day Easthorpe is a small hamlet on the south eastern  outskirts of Bottesford it appears that, in the 19th Century, Easthorpe was all of the area of Bottesford south of the River Devon. The Church was in Bottesford, Church Street in Easthorpe. This would explain why the 19th Century Census Returns for Easthorpe show that its population was much larger that its present size would indicate.

The next mention of Mary is her Marriage Certificate. She married William Marshall on 1st July 1850 at the Parish Church in the Parish of St Mary, Nottingham. When she married she was living at an address at High Pavement, Nottingham. This seems to have been a residential street in the centre of Nottingham and it is possible that she had been in service in one of the houses in that area. At the time of her marriage she was not working as she was 8 months pregnant.

Six weeks after her marriage her first child, Thomas, was born , on 19th August 1850 at Easthorpe.

Mary is next mentioned in the 1861 Census. She was now 36 years old and living with her husband William and their 5 children at The Beershop, Easthorpe, Bottesford. Also living with them was her mother Ann Lamb.  It is possibly that they were living at the premises where her father had been the Publican before he died.
