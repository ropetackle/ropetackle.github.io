Relationship: Thomas Marshall is a Great-Great-Great-Grandfather of Chloe and Daniel Marshall.
 
Born: 1850 in Bottesford, Leicestershire
 
Parents: William Marshall and Mary Lamb
 
Brothers and Sisters: William,  John,  George,  Ellen
 
Wife: Elizabeth Russell
 
Children:  George,  Mary,  Thomas,  William,  Thirza,  Elizabeth,  Charlotte,  Annie

Thomas Marshall was born at Easthorpe, Bottesford, Leicestershire, on the 19th August 1850. He was the first child of William Marshall, a Bricklayer, and Mary Lamb. He was Christened at Bottesford Church on the 23rd September 1850.

He had at least three brothers and one sister. These were:-

William, John, George and Ellen

He married Elizabeth Russell and they had 8 children. These were:-

George, Mary, Thomas, William, Thirza, Elizabeth, Charlotte and Annie

The earliest reference to Thomas is his Birth Certificate. When he was born his parents were living in Easthorpe, Bottesford and his father was a Bricklayer.

The next reference to him is in the 1861 Census. He was then 10 years old and living with his parents, brothers and sister, and maternal grandmother, Ann Lamb, at the Beershop, Easthorpe, Bottesford. It is possible that this was the licensed premises in Church Street, where his maternal Grandfather, Thomas Lamb, had been the Publican. There is currently a Public house, The Red Lion, on the corner of Church Street and Grantham Road which might have been where he was living.

When the 1881 Census was taken he was living at 5 Potts Square, Pierrepont Street in the Parish of St Marys, Nottingham. He was 30 years old, was married to Elizabeth Russell and, like his father, working as a Bricklayer. He and Elizabeth already had two children, George and Mary.

It was at Potts Square that his third child, Thomas, was born on 12th April 1881, nine days after the Census.

By the time of the 1891 Census the family had moved from Potts Square to a 4 roomed house at 6 Skelton Terrace, Hawthorne Street, Wilford, Nottingham. Thomas was now 40 years old, still a Bricklayer and there were four more children, William, Thirza, Elizabeth, and Charlotte.
The house appears to have been a typical C19th working class house, probably a “2 up and 2 down” and Conditions in the house must have been crowded as, in addition to the 9 members of the Marshall family, there was also a 16 year old lodger, Samuel Featherstone, a Coal Mine Pit Boy.

When the 1901 Census was taken, Thomas was 50 years old, still a Bricklayer, and still living in Hawthorn Street. By then he and Elizabeth had another child, Annie.
In the Census Return their address is given as 6 Cary Cottages and not 6 Skelton Terrace. It is not known if these were the same or different houses. However, in view of the fact that the numbers are the same and that the 1891 Census Returns do not mention there being any Cary Cottages in Hawthorne Street, it is almost certain that they were one and the same house.

When his son Thomas joined the Army in August 1901, both Thomas and Elizabeth were still living at 6 Cary Cottages.

Some time after his son Thomas joined the Army, Thomas may have either moved to 62 Briar Street, Meadows, Nottingham or died. The evidence for this is the change in the address of his Next-Of-Kin in Thomas's Army Small Book. This change probably took place at some time between 1901 and 1911, possibly round about 1903.

Very little is known about Thomas. His grandson, Thomas Robert Marshall, used to say that Thomas strongly disliked Jesse Boot, the man who founded Boots the Chemists, because Jesse never paid his bills. In particular he never paid for some building work that Thomas had carried out for him. Thomas apparently also claimed that it was by not paying his bills that Jesse made his fortune!
