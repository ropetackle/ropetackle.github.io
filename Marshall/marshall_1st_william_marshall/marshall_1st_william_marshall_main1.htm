Relationship: William Marshall is a Great-Great-Great-Great-Grandfather of Chloe and Daniel Marshall.
  Born: About 1824 or 1825 in Nottinghamshire
 
Parents: Thomas Marshall and Mrs Marshall
 
Wife: Mary Lamb
 
Children: Thomas,  William,  John,  George,  Ellen

William was the son of Thomas Marshall, a Farmer, and his wife.

He was born in 1824 or 1825 at a place called either Eaton, Eston or Exton in Nottinghamshire.

He married Mary Lamb in July 1850 and they had at least 5 children. These were:-

Thomas,  William,  John,  George and Ellen

It is not certain where William was born. In the 1861 Census he manuscript entry for his birth place in Nottinghamshire is hard to decipher and could be either Eaton, Eston or Exton.

The first reference to William is his Marriage Certificate. He married Mary Lamb by licence on 1stJuly 1850 at the Parish Church in the Parish of St. Mary, Nottingham. His Marriage Certificate describes him as a Bricklayer living at Bottesford, Leicestershire. Mary also came from Bottesford.

When William married Mary she was already 8 months pregnant and their first child, Thomas, was born at Easthorpe, Bottesford on 19th August 1850.

William is next mentioned in the 1861 Census. He, Mary and their 5 children were living at The Beershop, Easthorpe, Bottesford together with his widowed mother-in-law Ann Lamb. He was 36 years old and still working as a Bricklayer.
Although no further information is given as to their address, Mary's father, Thomas Lamb, had been a Publican and Victualler and in 1841 was living in Church Street, Easthorpe and it is possible that they were living at the premises where her father had been the Publican before he died. This might have been the Public House which is now known as the Red Lion and which is on the corner of Church Street and Grantham Road.
