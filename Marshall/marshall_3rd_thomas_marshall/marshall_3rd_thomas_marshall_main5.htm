Post War Service - 1919 To 1922

On its return from Germany in 1919, the remnants of the 1RSF went to Ayr and then Greenock where it was brought back up to strength. Later that year it moved to Bordon Camp, Hampshire.

In January 1921 the 1 RSF was sent to Ireland for operations in aid of the government during the Troubles and were stationed at Tullamore. Following the settlement with the Irish leaders the Battalion left Ireland in 1922.  

Thomas was with the Battalion in Ireland although he is thought to have returned early to England as a result of a leg injury. According to his son, he always said that although he had told the military officials that he and his group had been escaping from an attack by some Irish, they had actually been returning from a bar, drunk, when he fell over the edge of a quarry in the dark.

On its return from Ireland in April 1922 the Battalion moved to Glasgow and it was there that he was discharged from the Army on 9th August 1922 after completing 21 years service with the 1st Battalion Royal Scots Fusiliers.
