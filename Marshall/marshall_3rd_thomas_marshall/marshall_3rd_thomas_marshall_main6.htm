Civilian Life - 1922 To 1941

When Thomas left the Army in 1922 it was not a particularly good time to be looking for employment, especially in Glasgow. It was the height of the Depression and the level of unemployment was high. He and Florence had thought of getting work as the Steward and Stewardess at a club somewhere but this did not come about.

He was unemployed for some time but eventually he became employed as a Night Watchman at the Rolls Royce factory at Paisley, Glasgow. He obtained this employment through the Royal Corps Of Commissionaires. This organisation found work for former Non Commissioned Officers as Doormen, Receptionists and Night Watchmen at Offices and other commercial and industrial premises. In those days a Night Watchman was a combination of Gateman, Doorkeeper and Security Man.

When his son married in 1937 Thomas and Florence were living in Hillington, Glasgow at 83 Bearford Drive, and the Marriage Certificate gives his occupation as that of Watchman.

When he died in 1941, aged 60, Thomas was working as a Parts Inspector at an aircraft factory, this was almost certainly the Rolls Royce factory in Paisley.
